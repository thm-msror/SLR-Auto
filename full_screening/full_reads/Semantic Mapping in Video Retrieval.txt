- Notes: Thesis focusing on improving visual search effectiveness via semantic query-to-concept mapping for handling ad-hoc textual queries in video retrieval tasks.
- Related works: The paper builds upon prior research in video event detection, specifically addressing challenges in handling ad-hoc textual queries for high-level events like birthday parties. It draws inspiration from the security domain and aims to improve search capabilities beyond pre-trained concepts. The decompositionality assumption posits that high-level events can be decomposed into smaller pre-trained concepts, while the open world assumption acknowledges incomplete world knowledge. Current state-of-the-art systems struggle to handle the vast number of potential textual queries.

- Experiments: The study focuses on improving visual search effectiveness via a semantic query-to-concept mapping using three primary methods: i) Incremental Word2Vec (i-w2v), which leverages word2vec embeddings and incrementally selects concepts for queries to address query drift; ii) Adaptive Relevance Feedback (ARF), a theoretical foundationed method adjusting concept weights based on positive and negative annotations; and iii) Blind Late Fusion, exploring various combinations of state-of-the-art methods to fuse multiple modalities. Performance evaluation utilizes the TRECVID Multimedia Event Detection benchmark.

- Results: The i-w2v method significantly enhances Mean Average Precision (MAP) compared to existing word2vec and knowledge-based techniques when applied within a video event retrieval pipeline. ARF demonstrates superior performance over k-NN and concept level annotation methods. Blind Late Fusion, especially JRER, excels in situations where detectors are reliable, achieving high performance through probability ratio fusions.

- Limitations: Success depends on the availability of relevant concepts in the Concept Bank. Future directions involve deeper learning methods, integrating spatial and temporal information, and implicitly mapping queries to concepts.

- Notes: Minimalist transformer-based model for video grounding in the Ego4D Natural Language Queries Challenge. Achieves 12.64% Mean R@1 and ranks second on the public leaderboard.
- Related works: The paper reviews prior research on action recognition on small datasets, specifically mentioning methods utilizing CNNs and transfer learning. Prior studies on UCF101 demonstrate improvements in classification accuracy using frame-level feature extraction with 3D CNNs. Approaches incorporating multimodal cues, such as combining video with audio or text, were explored but often required large datasets. This work aims to provide a lightweight alternative suitable for scenarios with limited video data.

- Experiments and Methodology: The authors tested their CNN classifier on subsets of UCF101, varying network depths and frame sampling strategies. Comparing a standard 3D CNN against a shallower 2D CNN with temporal aggregation, the 3D CNN showed slightly better accuracy but at higher computational cost. Data augmentation with random cropping and horizontal flipping improved performance on smaller subsets. Audio features were not utilized, and attempts to include simple optical flow preprocessing did not significantly enhance results.

- Results: The 3D CNN achieved an average classification accuracy of 78% on the test subset, while the shallower 2D CNN reached 72%. Accuracy varied across action classes, with more complex actions like "handstand" performing worse due to fewer training examples. Precision and recall values of 0.76 and 0.74 respectively confirmed the model's ability to capture temporal information effectively on small datasets.

- Limitations: Performance is constrained by the size and diversity of the dataset; accuracy drops on classes with few examples. The model lacks incorporation of audio or multimodal features, limiting its application to static, audio-less videos. Computational efficiency is modest but could be further optimized for real-time inference. Generalization to larger, more complex datasets remains untested.

## Methods Summary

### Rising Methods

*Vision-Language Models (VLMs)* have seen an increase in usage from 2024 onwards. They were first mentioned in `Enhancing Subsequent Video Retrieval via Vision-Language Models` published in 2025. This method was also used in later publications like `Multi-event Video-Text Retrieval`.

### Stable Methods

*Self-Supervised Learning* has been consistently used since 2021 (`Self-supervised Video Retrieval Transformer Network`). It remains popular throughout the period covered by these studies.

*Transformer Architectures* have maintained their presence since 2021 (`Self-supervised Video Retrieval Transformer Network`) and continue to be employed in more recent works such as `Induce, Edit, Retrieve: Language Grounded Multimodal Schema for Instructional Video Retrieval`.

### Declining Methods

There doesn't seem to be a significant decline in popularity for specific methods within this timeframe. However, older techniques like *Heuristic Initialization* (`Video Editing for Video Retrieval`, published in 2024) might indicate less frequent adoption compared to newer approaches.

### Emerging Methods

The following methods appeared only once but were included in the latest papers:

*Graph Construction*: Mentioned in `Graph Based Temporal Aggregation for Video Retrieval` (published in 2020). Although relatively old, its appearance again in `Not All Pairs are Equal: Hierarchical Learning for Average-Precision-Oriented Video Retrieval` suggests renewed interest.

*Large Language Models (LLMs)*: First noted in `Towards Retrieval Augmented Generation over Large Video Libraries` (published in 2024) and then further explored in `Bridging Information Asymmetry in Text-video Retrieval: A Data-centric Approach` (published in 2024). Their increasing role indicates emerging importance.
